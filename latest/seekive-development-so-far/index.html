A Look at Seekive Development So Far

Seekive
March 10, 2026
Development

Seekive has been in development for a while now, and we wanted to take a moment to share where things stand — what's been built, how it looks, and where it's heading.

From the very beginning, the goal has been to create something that feels genuinely different. Not just another recommendations app, but a discovery experience that responds to who you are, where you are, and how you feel right now. The SpinWheel compass interface is at the heart of that — a custom-built interaction that makes finding your next activity feel playful rather than transactional.

Under the hood, Seekive is built in React Native with a Firebase backend, integrating Google Gemini AI to power real-time, personalised recommendations. Every feature — social, achievements, journal, weather awareness, monetisation — has been designed and built from scratch, solo.

🎨 AI-Generated Imagery

One of the things that makes Seekive visually distinct is its imagery. Rather than using stock photography, every activity image in the app has been produced using AI image generation workflows — crafted with custom prompts to visually represent specific activities, moods, and environments.

This approach keeps the visual language consistent and on-brand, while giving full creative control over how each experience is represented.

Each image was produced using AI generation tools, guided by prompts designed to capture the mood and setting of a specific activity category within the app.

AI has been woven into every layer of the project — from generating imagery, to planning features, drafting content, and powering the recommendation engine itself. It's not a bolt-on; it's core to how Seekive was built.

🚀 What's Next

Development is ongoing and an early release is coming. The core experience is in place — the SpinWheel, the AI recommendations, the social layer, the journal. What comes next is continued refinement and getting it into people's hands.
